Language Devices

Metaphor

Direct comparison (no "like" or "as")

Simile

Comparison using "like" or "as"

Personification

Giving human traits to non-human things

Alliteration

Repetition of the same starting consonant

Sibilance

Repetition of "s" or "sh" sounds for effect

Juxtaposition

Placing two contrasting ideas side-by-side

Oxymoron

Two contradictory words together

Hyperbole

Exaggeration for emphasis

Onomatopoeia

Words that imitate sound

Triplet

Three ideas listed for impact

Emotive Language

Words that stir strong feelings

Pathetic Fallacy

Nature mirroring mood or events

Irony

Saying the opposite of what is meant, or when the outcome is the opposite of what's expected

Symbolism

A thing representing something else

Structural Devices

Repetition

Repeating words

Flashback / Flashforward

Interrupting the chronological order to show past/future events

Foreshadowing

Hints about what's to come

Zoom In / Zoom Out

Changes in focus — from macro to micro (or reverse)

Circular Structure

Text begins and ends in the same or similar way

Shift

Sudden change in tone, mood, or direction

Withholding Information

Not telling the reader everything — builds mystery or suspense
