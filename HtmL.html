English Language Analysis Guide

Structures for Q2, Q3, Q4 & Letter Writing

✅ Q2: Compare Ideas/Experiences

No language analysis – focus on content and perspectives

Structure:
P (Focus) → BP (Text B's View) → AP (Text A's View)
→ A-E-L (x3) → Connective → B-E-L (x3)

Template:

P: The two texts show different [e.g., experiences of city life]. BP: Text B presents it as [Text B's overall view]. AP: However, Text A presents a different view, where the writer... A-E-L 1: In Text A, the writer says, "QUOTE A1". This suggests... (Explain). Links to the question because... A-E-L 2: Another quote, "QUOTE A2", shows... (Explain). Links because... A-E-L 3: The quote "QUOTE A3" implies... (Explain). Connects to the focus because... Connective: In contrast / On the other hand / Meanwhile B-E-L 1: In Text B, the writer says, "QUOTE B1". This shows... (Explain). Links because... B-E-L 2: Another example is "QUOTE B2", which implies... (Explain). Supports the idea that... B-E-L 3: Finally, "QUOTE B3" reveals... (Explain). Links back to the question because...

✅ Q3: Language Analysis

Analyze how writers create effects

Structure:
AP → R-T-E-Z-E-L (x3 paragraphs)

Template:

AP: The writer presents [feeling/mood/atmosphere] in the text. Paragraph 1: - R: "QUOTE 1" - T: Uses [technique] - E: Creates [effect] - Z: Zooming in on "WORD" reveals... - E: Strengthens the message by... - L: Links to the question because... Connective: Furthermore / Additionally Paragraph 2: - R: "QUOTE 2" - T: Uses [technique] - E: Suggests... - Z: The phrase "___" emphasises... - E: Gives the impression that... - L: Connects to the focus as it shows... Connective: Finally / Moreover Paragraph 3: - R: "QUOTE 3" - T: [Technique] is used - E: The effect is... - Z: The word "___" is powerful because... - E: Helps the reader understand... - L: Relates to the question because...

✅ Q4: Compare Perspectives/Attitudes

Compare writers' viewpoints and techniques

Structure:
AP-R-T-E → PB-R-T-E → Connective (Repeat x3)

Template:

Paragraph 1: - AP: In Text A, the writer presents [attitude/idea]. - R: "QUOTE A1" - T: [Technique] - E: Suggests... - PB: In Text B, the writer shows a [similar/different] idea. - R: "QUOTE B1" - T: [Technique] - E: Implies... Connective: However / Whereas Paragraph 2: - AP: Text A also presents [idea]. - R: "QUOTE A2" - T: [Technique] - E: Shows... - PB: In contrast, Text B says... - R: "QUOTE B2" - T: [Technique] - E: Gives the impression that... Connective: Similarly / In addition Paragraph 3: - AP: Another attitude in Text A is... - R: "QUOTE A3" - T: [Technique] - E: Highlights... - PB: Meanwhile, Text B shows... - R: "QUOTE B3" - T: [Technique] - E: Shows that...

✅ Q5: Formal Letter Writing

Structure and templates for persuasive letters

Format Reminder:
  • Sender's address (skip in exam)
  • Recipient's name and title (e.g. Councillor Smith)
  • Date (optional in exam)
  • Greeting (e.g. Dear Councillor Smith,)
  • Intro paragraph
  • Main argument paragraphs (PEEL/PEAL structure)
  • Closing paragraph
  • Sign-off (Yours sincerely / Yours faithfully)
  • Your name (e.g. ShadowRoot17 😎)

🔥 Introduction Template

Dear [Title + Surname], I am writing to express my strong [support/opposition] regarding [insert topic, e.g. the plan to introduce AI surveillance in public areas]. As a young person who is deeply affected by this issue, I believe it is vital that voices like mine are taken seriously. Decisions like this will impact not just today's society, but the future we are all heading into — and I refuse to remain silent. Whether you realise it or not, this matter is already shaping the way we think, feel, and live. For this reason, I am urging you to [support/stop/change] this proposal before it causes lasting damage.

🔥 Closing Paragraph Template

In conclusion, I hope you reflect on the real impact this decision will have on people like me. This is not just a political debate — it's personal. If we want to move forward as a society that values fairness, safety, and the future, then the right choices must be made today. I respectfully ask that you reconsider your position on [insert topic], and take the voices of young people seriously. We are not asking for favours — just for a future worth believing in. Yours sincerely, [Your Name Here]

⚠️ Extra Style Tips:

- Use direct address ("you", "your") - Keep the tone formal but passionate - Use rhetorical questions, triples, and strong verbs - Paragraphs should be structured like PEEL or PEAL - Show awareness of audience (the person in power) and purpose (to persuade)
